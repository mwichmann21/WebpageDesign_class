Shanghai Tunnels

Portland, Oregon

History

The Shanghai tunnels are the infamous Portland underground tunnels. These tunnel hold a reputation of being the center of illegal activities and business for the Chinese community.

The first wave of Chinese immigrants entered Portland in the 1850s. During this time there were high anti-Chinese sentiment across America against Chinese laborers. It is believed that the tunnels were used to help move product to and from the waterfront. They tunnels connected to stores, and therefore they were able to keep off the streets.

Portland used their tunnels in daylight hours to deliver goods and businesses but during the evening hours, there was horrible criminal activity. There was drugs, prostitution, gambling and illegal booze.

Criminal Activity

There was drugs, prostitution, gambling and illegal booze. Trafficking and sex trade led to many deaths under the streets.

Portland developed a reputation for being a gritty port town in the late 1800s. A number of illegal activities occurred during this time were found in the tunnels. There were secret stairways, doors and tunnels. Portland was living up its reputation of being a violent and dangerous city. There was illegal dealings of drug. There was also illegal gambling. Gambling would occur in gambling dens that were in the tunnels. During the prohibition era, these tunnels and basements provided a convenient way conduct illegal selling and consumption of alcohol without being caught. Illegal boxing was popular during the Prohibition Era. Matches were held in unlikely places, the Shanghai tunnels was a popular place to conduct matches because they were out of the rain and were unseen by everyday businesses. Illegal saloons were also conveniently held in the tunnels.

1850-1941: Human Trafficking

Ship Captains hired vicious thugs called crimps to spot men new to town. They would buy and the drink and add drugs to it before giving it to them. They would drop them down into the tunnels. A bounty was paid of 50 dollars but the crimps had to cram into small wooden cages without food or drink. After a couple days they were given another drug that would allow them move to the ships after being sold to various Captains. Crimps also befriended young women and imprisoned them in tunnels. After being kidnapped they were kept in isolation in a box alone. Within the next couple hours or days, they were convinced that they were forced into sex trade.

By 1941, human trafficking of men and women became unprofitable so the practice were stopped entirely. They stayed off limites until the Cascade Historial Society discovered them. Now they give tours.

Things to do!!!

Take a tour! There is many different tours.

The tours are offered by The Cascade Historical Society and The Portland Walking Tours. The Cascade Historical Society offers three different kinds of tours that begin at Hobos Restaurant.

  1. The Shanghai Tunnels Heritage Tour - This is the main tour given. This tour offers the history of the shanghai trade in Portland. They offer evening tours.
  2. The Shanghai Tunnels Ghost Tour - This tor gives accounts of the spirits still working and imprisoned in here. This is a great tour for a haunted Halloween.
  3. Shanghai Tunnel Ethnic History Tour - This tour talks about the historical stories of the Chinese, the Japanese and the Gypsies and their activities in the Portland underground community.

History of Manifestations

Manifestations are all around

"People who experience living in captivity, suffering at the hands of others, and dying in their circumstances, sometimes can't let go of their ordeal, and restlessly haunt the place of captivity"

Many captives died in the Portland Tunnels' underground cells due to harsh treatment, lack o food and water and overdoses of drugs. Terrified people who are killed by their pursuers sometimes can't let go of their trauma, and haunt the area where they were killed.


Entity of Nina

Haunts the Old Town Pizza that is located in what was The Merchant Hotel's Lobby.

Her picture is hanging on the wall and there is also a brick with her name etched into it. She is regarded as a brave heroine who dared to turn in her captors. She makes her presence know by appearing as a lady in white or a lady wearing a black dress. She likes to make her appearances in front of staff and patrons for her personal amusement. The aroma of her perfume is noticed by the living. She is most active in the early morning of closing time where she is floating and moving through the restaurant, moving chairs, items on the tables and kitchen to let employees know that she is there.


Entities of the Shanghaied crew of The Jennifer Jo

Haunts the entire tunnels during the anniversary of their drowning

Starting at the tunnels nearest the water, every year they haunt the tunnels as a group, looking for those responsible and hoping to get justice of some sort. Various witnesses have seen and heard them, some have actually been touched by a COLD, WET HAND!

There are many other human manifestations that haunt the Shanghai Tunnels providing a horrifying experience.
